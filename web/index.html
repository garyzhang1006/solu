The chemistry, in plain words

Solubility is a tug-of-war: does a molecule "want" to be surrounded by water, or to hide from it? Three forces decide most of it.

🧬

Like dissolves like

Water is polar. Polar, charged, or hydrogen-bonding molecules feel at home in it; greasy hydrocarbons get pushed out, the way oil beads on water.

🔗

Hydrogen bonds pull it in

-OH and -NH groups (donors) and O/N atoms (acceptors) latch onto water molecules. More of them, more solubility. That's why sugar dissolves and benzene doesn't.

⚖️

Size and grease push it out

High LogP (lipophilicity), big molecular weight, and flat aromatic rings all make a molecule prefer fat over water. Watch LogP dominate the bars above.

How it works

A real machine-learning pipeline — trained offline on published data, shipped to your browser. The prediction needs no server, no LLM, no API calls. An optional AI tutor (Claude Sonnet 4.6, via a local proxy) adds the plain-English “why” on top.

STEP 1

Read structure

RDKit-JS (WASM) parses your SMILES and computes 9 interpretable molecular descriptors.

STEP 2

Run the model

A gradient-boosting regressor (exported to ONNX) runs via ONNX Runtime Web, fully client-side.

STEP 3

Predict logS

It outputs log solubility in mol/L, converted to a familiar mg/L value and a qualitative class.

STEP 4

Explain

An interpretable linear model decomposes the result into per-property contributions you can learn from.
